What is Prefix and Postfix?

Prefix calculations usually imply that the operators precede the numbers, while Postfix is the opposite. Let's see some examples:

If we were to write the equation 9 + (2 * 3), in Prefix we would write +9 *23, which reads from left to right that 2 and 3 will be multiplied, then added to 9.

On the other hand, in Postfix we would write 923 *+, which reads from the middle to reach the outside, where we multiply 2 and 3 (inside), then add the result to 9.

Some Examples For You To try

Prefix Examples

  • Expression: + 5 4
    Result: 9
  • Expression: * 9 4
    Result: 36
  • Expression: * + 2 5 + 6 7
    Result: 91

Postfix Examples

  • Expression: 5 4 +
    Result: 9
  • Expression: 5 7 + 6 7 + *
    Result: 156
  • Expression: 9 2 3 *+
    Result: 15
